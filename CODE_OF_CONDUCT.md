# Erp.Toolkit Code of Conduct

Erp.Toolkit is an open-source community that upholds the spirit of collaboration, respect, and inclusiveness. We are committed to building a welcoming and safe environment where every participant, regardless of background, identity, or experience, can freely communicate and collaborate. This Code of Conduct aims to articulate our shared values and expectations, ensuring that everyone can contribute to Erp.Toolkit with mutual respect and professionalism.

## Respect and Inclusiveness

1.  Respect every community member, listen carefully to, and consider their perspectives and experiences.
2.  Use friendly and inclusive language, avoiding any form of demeaning, offensive, or discriminatory remarks.
3.  Be open to constructive feedback and differing viewpoints, engaging in thoughtful discussions and debates.
4.  Respect others' privacy and personal boundaries; always obtain consent before sharing personal information.
5.  Value and appreciate the contributions of every member, regardless of their expertise level or background.

## Fostering a Positive, Collaborative Environment

1.  Support others based on respect, encouraging a culture of teamwork and shared success.
2.  Show patience and understanding when assisting others, offering helpful guidance and care.
3.  Be mindful of the impact of your words and actions, striving to create a positive and harmonious environment for the community.
4.  Resolve conflicts constructively, addressing differences through peaceful negotiation to maintain community harmony and stability.
5.  Keep discussions focused on the technical level, avoiding personal attacks or off-topic debates.

## Respecting Intellectual Property and Licensing

1.  Ensure all contributions (including code, documentation, and media assets) comply with relevant licenses and copyright regulations.
2.  Respect and acknowledge the intellectual property of others; provide proper attribution and credit when using or referencing their work.
3.  Clearly indicate modifications or adaptations made to existing code or content.
4.  Encourage the use of open-source licenses to promote collaboration and resource sharing within the Erp.Toolkit community.

## Reporting and Enforcement

1.  If you observe any behavior that violates this Code of Conduct, please report it to the Erp.Toolkit community administrators via email at andy@doipc.com or andy@erpking.net.
2.  All reports will be handled with strict confidentiality. Administrators will investigate the reported incident and take appropriate action based on the findings.
3.  Participants who violate this Code of Conduct may face consequences, including temporary or permanent exclusion from community spaces and activities.

Remember, this Code of Conduct is designed to foster a positive and inclusive community environment. We expect every participant in Erp.Toolkit to uphold these principles and contribute collectively to creating a warm, harmonious, and vibrant community atmosphere.
